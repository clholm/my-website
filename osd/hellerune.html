The Hellerune overdrive is a CD4049UBE overdrive similar to the Greedtone or Red Llama overdrives. Schematic and PCB files can be found on my

Github

.



Like the pedals that inspired it, the Hellerune overdrive is an overdrive pedal that (hopefully, I haven't built it yet, lol) works well with bass guitar. This pedal uses 4 op-amps: a non-inverting input buffer, an inverting op-amp for the Baxandall* tone control, another non-inverting input buffer, and then a final inverting op-amp for volume control and preserving the signal's phase.



The Hellerune overdrive has two footswitches - an on/off switch and a "more" switch that reduces the input resistance to the CD4049UBE chip by switching resistors, thereby reducing the voltage drop experienced by the input signal to the CD4049UBE. I think that's how it works at least!



Version 1 of the pedal also uses an LT1054 chip to double the supply voltage for the op-amps and inverter. As an extra paranoid precaution, there's a voltage divider before the voltage doubler, just in case I use a "9V" power supply that runs hot. I use Strymon power supplies on my board, but for testing pedals I'll generally use a random power brick that I stole from my dad's electronic junk pile, and several of those output more than the 9V indicated.



I removed the voltage doubler chip in version 2 in case I ever want to sell some pedals to my friends (only for the cost of the parts, this isn't a profit-seeking venture!) and I need to comply with FCC regulations.



The name "Hellerune" comes from the Old English word for

sorceress or witch
